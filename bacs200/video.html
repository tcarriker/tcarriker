Social Media Stokes Extremes

Social media can show us the best of us all or the worst of us all there is very little inbetween. Unfortunately, social media makes us hate one side or another more than we should ever hate someone, especially just for their beliefs. Not only does it make us politically extreme, but it also makes us view ourselves in extreme fashion as well. We need to avoid this by logging off and remembering we are all human beings who need a break.

Why We Need Freedom to Ring

Freedom, it's supposed to be our most sacred value in this country, but is it really? It's hard to say yes from my point of view. With mass protests and racial tensions high, how free are we? The Situation:

Early evidence of climate change: I am a stronfg proponent of free speech so regulating what can be said on social media is not ideal for me. That is why we all need to do our part by staying up to date and researching before posting!

How We Can do This: When you see some very sensationalist post on social media that pushes your point if view heavily, first you should research other relevant sources to insure what you are posting is true. Of course you should also verify that your source is reliable and not some heavily biased proganda. And third you should see if you can find articles on the subject written by someone with an opposing view to yours and see if there is any middle ground.

Regulating free speech is not our job but making sure you aren't spreading the fire of misinformation is!
